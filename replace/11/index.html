#arashiyamabambooforest | @wonderbywander
Bamboo-zled
Walking into the Arashiyama Bamboo Grove is an experience like no other. One main path winds through the soaring stalks and you can’t help but feel minuscule. To fully experience serenity, make a sunrise or sunset trip and leave the mid-day heat to the tour groups.
#kyototravel | @avantikamathur
Rollin, Rollin on the River
All aboard! Take a two-hour cruise down the Hozugawa river: home to one-of-a-kind rock formations, flourishing wildlife, and light rapids. End the trip in Arashiyama, where you can visit the Monkey park, Tenryuji temple, and famous Bamboo forest!
#giondistrict | @nagimariequirk
All Dressed up with A City  to Explore
Rent a kimono and live like a local in the Gion district. Grab a cup of tea at an “Ochaya” (traditional tea house), explore the many shops in the area, and keep an eye out for one of the Geisha that call this area of Kyoto home.
#kyotomatcha  | @phanyyen
Matcha Talkin' About
Matcha tea is a local specialty of Kyoto, and while you can grab a glass of the green goodness at any of the local tea shops, the flavor shows up in plenty of other foods too. For example matcha kettle corn, matcha cake, matcha macaroons, and more. We recommend treating yourself to some matcha soft serve on your treks between shrines.

Ryokan Royals
When in Japan, live like the Japanese in a traditional ryokan. And our top pick in Kyoto is Shiraume. Housed in a traditional Gion ochaya building that’s minutes from the top tourist spots, this ryokan offers a full immersive experience. Dress in a yukata and sip tea while the owner tells you about Japanese culture.

The Wheels on the Bus
Strap on your walking shoes and grab a map. Discoverer Marinette learned that the best way to get around Kyoto is by traveling like a local.
"While trains are your main transportation, you will have to get on the bus and walk no matter what. It may be less convenient, but it will get you around the city. If you're a walker, this city is for you! So factor in travel times when you plan a trip. That's what ate a lot of our extra time (plus, the tempting ice cream stands on every corner)."
